LIONESS




PERSISTENT DEPRESSIVE DISORDER WITH SUICIDAL TENDENCIES

.


As you may or may not know the month of May is Mental Illness Awareness month and so it just seemed fit it have this discussion today. Specifically depression, now what is depression? According to the American Psychiatric Association depression is described as:

“Depression (major depressive disorder) is a common and serious medical illness that negatively affects how you feel, the way you think and how you act. Fortunately, it is also treatable. Depression causes feelings of sadness and/or a loss of interest in activities once enjoyed. It can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems and can decrease a person’s ability to function at work and at home.”

Now the key words in this definition are ‘serious medical illness’. Serious. I feel like this needs to be emphasized because most people take the idea of a mental illness lightly. Just because the effects are not immediately visible doesn’t mean that they don’t exist. There are stigmas out there like this that makes it hard for most people to seek help or even accept help. Mental Illness is real, Depression is real.

There are many different types and levels of depression. The 1 I will be talking about today is Persistent Depressive Disorder with Suicidal tendencies (PDD). It is also known as Dysthymia. This a chronic form of depression that is more than just about feeling sad it causes many people to not only think about death and suicide but they also tend to practice self-mutilation.



• Over 25 million Americans suffer from depression every year.

• Depression affects nearly 5-8 percent of Americans ages 18 and over in a given year.

• Over 50 percent of all people who die by suicide suffer from major depression.

• Close to 800 000 people die due to suicide every year, this is one person every 40 seconds.

• Suicide is the second leading cause of death among 15-29 year olds globally.

• 78% of suicides occurred in low- and middle-income countries in 2015.

• Suicide accounted for 1.4% of all deaths worldwide, making it the 17th leading cause of death in 2015.

• In 2016 there were 44,965 reported suicide deaths.

• Currently, suicide is the 10th leading cause of death in the United States.

• A person dies by suicide about every 11.7 minutes in the United States.

• Every day, approximately 123 Americans take their own life.

• Ninety percent of all people who die by suicide have a diagnosable psychiatric disorder at the time of their death.

• There are 3.6 male suicides for every female suicide, but twice as many females as males attempt suicide.

• According to the latest available data 494,169 people visited a hospital for injuries due to self-harm, suggesting that approximately 12 people harm themselves for every reported death by suicide

• Suicide was the second leading cause of death for adults between the ages of 10 and 34 years in the United States.
